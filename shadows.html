Happy Makar Sankranti!

Makar Sankranti is a festival celebrated in India to mark the transition of the sun into the zodiac sign of Capricorn (Makara). It usually falls on January 14th each year and is considered a harvest festival. The festival is known by different names in various regions of India, such as Pongal in Tamil Nadu, Uttarayan in Gujarat, and Maghi in Punjab. Makar Sankranti is celebrated with various customs and traditions, including kite flying, bonfires, feasting on traditional sweets made from sesame seeds and jaggery, and taking holy dips in rivers. It is a time of joy and thanksgiving for a bountiful harvest.
